3 Automation Wins I Delivered for My Team Last Quarter

Real automation projects that saved time, reduced errors, and made the team's work more meaningful.

I believe in showing, not telling. So here are three automation projects I delivered for my team in the last quarter — what the problem was, what I built, and what happened.

1. Automated Incident Triage

The problem: Our service desk was spending 20+ minutes per incident just categorizing, prioritizing, and assigning tickets. The process was manual, inconsistent, and a frequent source of delays.

What I built: A small AI-powered script that reads the incident description and automatically assigns priority, category, and the right assignment group. It uses a lightweight model fine-tuned on our historical ticket data.

The result: Triage time dropped from ~20 minutes to under 2 minutes. Accuracy improved because the model was more consistent than humans at classifying edge cases. The team went from dreading triage duty to handling it as a background task.

2. Weekly Report Generator

The problem: Every Friday, team leads spent 45-60 minutes compiling weekly status reports. They'd pull data from multiple systems, format it, and write summaries. It was tedious, error-prone, and nobody enjoyed reading them either.

What I built: A simple tool that connects to our monitoring and ticketing systems via APIs, pulls the key metrics, and generates a formatted report using AI. The team lead just adds a few bullet points of context, and the tool does the rest.

The result: Report creation dropped to under 10 minutes. The reports became more consistent and data-rich. One team lead told me it saved her "the worst hour of the week."

3. Automated Onboarding Checklist

The problem: Onboarding a new team member required 30+ steps across 8 different systems — account creation, permissions, distribution lists, tool access, documentation, and more. It was manual, took 3-5 hours per person, and mistakes were common.

What I built: An automated workflow that triggers when an onboarding request is submitted. It provisions accounts, sends notifications, creates tracking tickets, and generates a personalized onboarding guide — all without manual intervention.

The result: Onboarding time dropped from 3-5 hours to about 30 minutes of oversight. Error rate dropped to near zero. New hires got access on day one instead of day three.

What These Have in Common

Looking at these three wins, the pattern is clear:

  • Each solved a specific, recurring pain point the team already felt
  • Each was small enough to build in days, not months
  • Each freed up time for higher-value work — not just "efficiency for efficiency's sake"

You don't need a big budget or a dedicated automation team to deliver this kind of impact. You just need to look at what your team does repeatedly, pick the most painful thing, and build the simplest solution that could possibly work.
